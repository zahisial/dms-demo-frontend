Web Privacy Policy

Edarat Filing Management System

Last Updated: January 28, 2024 • Effective Date: January 28, 2024

Important: This privacy policy governs the use of the Edarat Filing Management System (FMS) and describes how we collect, use, protect, and share your personal information.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

We collect the following personal information when you use our system:

  • Identity Information: Name, employee ID, email address, department, and role
  • Authentication Data: Microsoft Teams SSO credentials and session tokens
  • Profile Information: Profile pictures, contact details, and organizational hierarchy
  • Communication Data: Messages, notifications, and system communications

1.2 Document and Usage Information

  • Document Data: Files uploaded, document metadata, version history, and approval workflows
  • Activity Logs: Login times, document access, download history, and system interactions
  • Search Data: Search queries, filter preferences, and usage patterns
  • Technical Information: IP addresses, browser type, device information, and session data

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 Primary Purposes

  • System Operation: Provide and maintain the document management system
  • User Authentication: Verify identity and manage secure access
  • Document Management: Store, organize, and facilitate document workflows
  • Collaboration: Enable document sharing and approval processes
  • Compliance: Meet regulatory requirements and audit obligations

2.2 Secondary Purposes

  • System Improvement: Analyze usage patterns to enhance functionality
  • Security: Monitor for unauthorized access and security threats
  • Support: Provide technical assistance and troubleshooting
  • Reporting: Generate usage statistics and compliance reports

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

3.1 Internal Sharing

Information may be shared within Edarat Group for:

  • Legitimate business operations and document workflows
  • Compliance with internal policies and procedures
  • System administration and technical support
  • Audit and quality assurance purposes

3.2 External Sharing

We may share information with third parties only in the following circumstances:

  • Legal Compliance: When required by law, regulation, or court order
  • Service Providers: With vetted vendors who assist in system operations (under strict confidentiality agreements)
  • Business Transfers: In the event of merger, acquisition, or sale of assets
  • Emergency Situations: To protect safety, prevent harm, or investigate suspected illegal activities

4. Data Security

4.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption: Data encrypted in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols
  • Access Controls: Role-based permissions and multi-factor authentication
  • Network Security: Firewalls, intrusion detection, and secure network architecture
  • Regular Updates: Security patches and system updates applied promptly

4.2 Administrative Safeguards

  • Staff Training: Regular security awareness training for all personnel
  • Access Management: Principle of least privilege and regular access reviews
  • Incident Response: Established procedures for security breach response
  • Vendor Management: Due diligence and security assessments for third-party providers

5. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, including:

  • Active Employment: Throughout the duration of employment or system access
  • Document Lifecycle: Based on document retention policies and legal requirements
  • Audit Logs: Maintained for 7 years for compliance and security purposes
  • Legal Obligations: As required by applicable laws and regulations

When information is no longer needed, it will be securely deleted or anonymized in accordance with our data destruction procedures.

6. Your Rights

6.1 Access and Portability

  • Right to Access: Request copies of your personal information
  • Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Information Requests: Learn about how your data is processed

6.2 Correction and Control

  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal and business requirements)
  • Right to Restriction: Limit how your information is processed
  • Right to Object: Opt-out of certain types of processing

7. Cookies and Tracking

Our system uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Authentication: Maintain secure login sessions
  • Preferences: Remember user settings and preferences
  • Analytics: Understand system usage and performance
  • Security: Detect and prevent unauthorized access

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings, though disabling certain cookies may affect system functionality.

8. International Data Transfers

If data is transferred outside your country of residence, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Adequacy Decisions: Transfers to countries with adequate data protection
  • Standard Contractual Clauses: EU-approved transfer mechanisms
  • Binding Corporate Rules: Internal data transfer agreements
  • Consent: Explicit consent when required by law

9. Updates to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy periodically to reflect:

  • Changes in our data processing practices
  • Updates to applicable laws and regulations
  • New features and system enhancements
  • Feedback from users and stakeholders

Material changes will be communicated through system notifications and email alerts. Continued use of the system after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
